5 Oct 2014

I changed jobs recently and had to come to grips with not having a smart phone for the first time in 5+ years.

I changed in the middle of summer just after Google I/O and before the iphone 6/6+ came out. I decided to wait and see which one I would get. In the mean time I decided to buy the simplest phone i could. The only requirement was that it was charged via micro usb.

The only candidate i could find was a samsung flip phone. it was by far better than the first mobile phone i had but not by much. I can call and send text messages and that’s about it.

it’s been an interesting couple of months and i don’t know how long this little experiment it going to continue. Some of the things i have obesrved:

  • I notice how much everybody else is on their phone.

  • Not being able to get to mail has been good - at least until the kids school started.

  • My kids don’t know how to use t-9. which has been hilarious at times.

  • need to keep a map in the car

  • can’t get to my passwords at all when i am not near a computer

  • Only have to charge it every other week and it charges in max 45 minutes

  • Can’t take pictures - don’t carry a camera. Have missed out on some of those little life moments not being able to take a picture/video

  • i need to use a computer more often - to check mail and what not. i also get on the computer and off a lot more quickly.

  • I carry my kindle around more often than not.

I don’t know how long this is going to last but probably not much longer it has helped that i have starting riding my bike to and from work most days.
