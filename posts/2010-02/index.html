buildr jruby rake+ant

must be synchronicity but with in the last couple of days i have seen some new/old build tools for java. The first is jruby’s new rake and ant integration seen on engine yard’s blog. If you are using ant and jruby this looks like you can get the best of both worlds as you can call ant from rake and rake from ant. All with out getting swept away…. The other build tool seen was when attended a presentation tonight on buildr given by Anders Janmyr at jayway’s office.

the passionate-programmer

finished the passionate programmer yesterday. It’s been 6 years since i read the pragmatic programmer and from what i remember of it the passionate programmer is a great companion book. This must be one of the first books i have read from some one that has had many different roles in the software industry. Chad Fowler has been a programmer, manager, CTO, headhunter and consultant. The chapters were quite short and focused with homework/tips/assignments at the end of most of the chapters.

ruby best practices

finished reading Gregory T. Brown’s ruby best practices. After reading programming ruby: the pragmatic programmers guide a couple of years ago and the ruby way in the spring of 2009 it’s definitely a worth while read and is going to be well thumbed threw. I really liked that the book started off with a whole chapter on unit testing. Chapters 2 and 3 were a little difficult but mainly because i don’t use ruby enough daily these chapters will definitely mean more study :).
