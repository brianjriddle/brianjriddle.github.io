24 Mar 2013

I have been using google reader since at least december 2008. It was a great way to stay on top of the current event’s in my field as well as bloggers i find interesting. the biggest draw google reader had was that it was available on every platform i used since it used a web based interface which meant i could use it on computer at work, home and my mobile. after using it for a couple of weeks i had abandoned liferea.

fast forward to december 2012. i had started using mutt pretty exclusively for my private email(would love to use it at work but they won’t enable imap on exchange) and read zed shaw I Want The Mutt Of Feed Readers. show i started testing newsbeuter at home and really liked the work flow i have with it but at the time i could not come up with a way to make it work across different machines (home, work, phone) and have every thing in sync.

then google announced the were sunsetting reader. i have to admit i started to panic as i use google reader a lot. i looked at some of the services that competed with google reader feedly, newsblur but decided to take another look at newsbeuter.

so the past 2 weeks i have been testing newsbeuter out and can say that i enjoy this as much or better than google reader. to get around the synchronization problem i found that you can configure where newsbeuter keeps it’s cache. it’s an sqlite3 database and i put it in my Dropbox account. the only thing this doesn’t fix is that i can’t access this on my phone but that is ok as i want to use my phone less

my configuration is here

newsbeuter/config

i have also added a small script to add an entry to my pinboard account

newsbeuter/bin/bookmark-pinboard.rb

pretty happy with how this setup is working even if there are a couple of things that take a little more time - such as viewing picture blogs like http://devopsreactions.tumblr.com/. it entails opening up a browser to see the content but for reading and bookmarking it’s a hella of a lot faster.
