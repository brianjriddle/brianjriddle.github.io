20 Mar 2010

Today we got an early start which was good as we were before the curve most of the day as a result. The Charles bridge was desolate at 08:00.

One of the nicest things about Prague is all the different architecture you can see just walking around in the old town and its surrounding areas. Everything from Gothic

jugend

cubist

modern

We were lucky to have a chance to see an exhibit of Alfons Muchas. I did not recognize his name at first but did easily recognize his but quick saw that I had seen quite a bit of his work before.

After the exhibition we had lunch at the cubista a Czech cubist restaurant with decor from when cubism was born.

More wandering after lunch and a quick trip back to the hotel after finding a UK Tesco(My in Prague) and doing some quick shopping. Walking around somethings stand out especially on the edges of the old town where the influence of the communist rule is rears it’s ugly head

And the graffiti that abounds such as xkcd copy

And the Lennon wall

When in Prague it would a crime to not at least try to catch a classical music concert or go to a jazz club, which there are quite a few. We lucked out and found a string quintet from the Czech philharmonic at the music school. We booked tickets and enjoyed a greatest hits of classical(Bach, Vivaldi, Mozart, Dvorak) music performed by and outstanding bunch of musicians. Even I liked it and I am not the biggest fan. Made Anneli promise that next time we would check out the jazz clubs.

We wandered around most of the rest of the day looking for a place to eat before the ghost walk we just had to go to. You know the ones we got free… I believe we ended up taking the first restaurant we could agree on once both our blood sugar had dropped enough and didn’t want to walk any more. I don’t know if it was the season (early spring) or if duck is a standard fare in the Czech republic but it was on most of the menus of the restaurants where we ate.

We were completely pooped after all the walking we had done during the day but we still managed the “ghost walk”. We walked somewhere between 27-36,000 steps and there was still tons we did not even get to.
